Free Home Assistant version

Your whole grocery and meal-planning flow, inside Home Assistant.

This version is for people who already run Home Assistant and want local-first list management directly inside HA. It has grown from a simple categorized list into a phone-first, one-stop hub: plan the week's meals, pull up a recipe's steps while you cook, and build the shopping list from it — all without leaving Home Assistant and without a separate cloud service or subscription.

What it is good at

  • Native, phone-first Home Assistant panel with a bottom tab bar — List, Shop, Meals, and Plan are each one tap away
  • Shopping Mode: a focused, big-tap-target screen in your store's aisle order for when you're actually shopping
  • Quick Add autocomplete that suggests items you've added before, each with its category already resolved
  • Saved meals with ingredients and numbered directions you can check off in cook mode
  • Import a meal straight from a recipe link — your HA reads the page and fills in the name, ingredients, and directions for you to review
  • Per-person meal favorites — each Home Assistant user stars their own go-to meals
  • Notes on any meal, plus category chips and a search box to find a meal by name or ingredient
  • A dated meal planner that builds a combined, de-duplicated shopping list for a day or a whole week
  • Smarter automatic categories with a checkbox list to turn sections on or off per list
  • Multiple local lists with color theming and your own per-list aisle order
  • Backup & restore to a single local JSON file, and fast local refresh across open clients

What's new

Recent releases turned the panel into a full meal-planning and shopping hub. Highlights:

Meals

Import from a recipe link

Paste a recipe URL into a new meal and tap Import — your Home Assistant reads the page's recipe data and fills in the name, ingredients, and directions for you to review and tweak before saving. It stays local: your HA fetches the page itself, nothing is sent to a cloud service.

Meals

Favorites, per person

Each Home Assistant user can star the meals they love. Favorites are personal to whoever's signed in and float to the top of the Meals list, so everyone in the house finds their go-to meals fast.

Meals

Notes, categories & search

Add notes to any meal — swaps, who likes it, serving ideas — right alongside the directions, and edit them in place without opening the editor. Give each meal a category (Dinner, Breakfast, Dessert, or your own) and filter the Meals tab with a tap, and search by name or ingredient as your collection grows.

Navigation

Phone-first bottom tab bar

List, Shop, Meals, and Plan sit in a bottom tab bar, so every main screen is one tap away instead of buried in a menu — built around how households actually use it on a phone.

At the store

Shopping Mode

A stripped-down screen that shows only the unchecked items, grouped in your list's category (store) order, with large tap targets, a progress bar, and a quick add for anything you forgot.

Faster entry

Quick Add autocomplete

As you type, a dropdown suggests items you've added before — each with its category already worked out — so a known item goes on the list in one tap. It also cuts down near-duplicates like "milk" vs "Milk".

Meals

Recipes with cook mode

Define a meal once — ingredients plus numbered directions. Add it with a pre-checked ingredient checklist, then switch to the Directions tab and tap each step off as you cook.

Planning

Dated meal planner

Assign saved meals to real dates, page between weeks, and add a single day's or the whole week's ingredients at once — combined and de-duplicated, shown in a confirm checklist. Nothing ever auto-clears.

Categories

Smarter, customizable

An expanded built-in knowledge of common items means far fewer things land in "Other," and a checkbox list lets each person turn categories on or off — because not everyone shops the same way.

Everything else it does

  • Frequent quick-add chips for the staples you add most often — no typing
  • Review-and-learn: teach the right category once for an item and it sticks
  • Duplicate decision flow (Add anyway or Skip) and a completed section with restore & clear
  • Drag-and-drop reordering of items and lists, and in-place category editing
  • Voice-friendly adds where Home Assistant provides user context, with per-user attribution
  • Follows your Home Assistant theme — light, dark, or custom
  • Installs through HACS; automatic provisioning and repair of the helpers and to-do lists it needs

Who should use it

Use the Home Assistant version if Home Assistant is already the place where your household automations, dashboards, and voice flows live. Use the Android app if you want a standalone product with optional sync and no Home Assistant dependency.

Cost

The Home Assistant version is free and local-only. The standalone Android app is a separate product with free local mode and optional paid sync.
